Local SEO Strategies That Actually Work

Skip the recycled checklists. These are the local SEO strategies we deploy every day for real clients — citation building, review velocity, and location page architecture that drives real customers.

Local SEO isn't a smaller version of national SEO — it's its own discipline, governed by a distinct set of ranking signals centered on proximity, relevance, and prominence. Most businesses get the basics right and stop there. Here's what separates businesses that dominate their local market from ones stuck on page two.

Build a Real Citation Profile, Not Just a Listing

Citations — mentions of your business Name, Address, and Phone number across directories, industry sites, and local publications — are one of the clearest local ranking signals Google has. The goal isn't volume; it's consistency and relevance. A handful of accurate, industry-relevant citations outperform a hundred inconsistent, low-quality ones.

Create Location-Specific Content, Not Just Location Pages

A location page listing your address and phone number does almost nothing for rankings on its own. What works is genuinely useful, location-specific content: neighborhoods you serve, local case studies, region-specific FAQs. This gives Google real signal that the page is relevant to that specific area — and gives visitors an actual reason to trust you.

Treat Reviews as an Ongoing System, Not a One-Time Push

A burst of reviews followed by silence looks unnatural to both Google and prospective customers. Build a simple, repeatable system — automated review requests after every completed job or appointment — so new reviews arrive steadily every week, not in occasional spikes.

Match Content to "Near Me" and Service-Area Intent

Searchers increasingly use "near me" and hyper-local phrasing. Structure your content and metadata around actual local search intent — service + suburb or region combinations — rather than generic national keywords that don't reflect how local customers actually search.

For Multi-Location Businesses

Build a unique, individually optimized page for every location — never a single generic "locations" page. Each page needs its own GBP-matched content, local testimonials, and localized keywords to avoid pages competing against each other.

Track Rankings by Location, Not Just Overall Traffic

National traffic dashboards hide local performance. Track rank-tracking by specific suburb, city, and keyword combination so you can see exactly where you're winning and where competitors still have an edge — then focus effort where it will move the needle fastest.
