Common SEO Mistakes Businesses Make

Keyword stuffing, ignoring mobile, duplicate content, and neglecting Google Business Profile — these are the mistakes quietly capping your rankings. Here's how to spot and fix each one.

Most SEO underperformance isn't caused by some hidden algorithm penalty — it's caused by a handful of common, fixable mistakes that quietly cap growth for months or years. Here are the ones we see most often when auditing a new client's site.

1. Treating Google Business Profile as an Afterthought

For any local business, an incomplete, unmanaged GBP is one of the costliest mistakes possible — it's often the very first thing potential customers see. See our dedicated guide for the specific fixes that matter most here.

2. Keyword Stuffing Over Genuine Answers

Repeating a keyword unnaturally throughout a page doesn't help rankings anymore — it actively hurts readability and trust. Modern SEO rewards content that thoroughly and naturally answers the searcher's question, not content optimized around a phrase.

3. Ignoring Mobile Experience

Google predominantly indexes and ranks based on your mobile site. A desktop-first website with a cramped, slow, or broken mobile experience is fighting an uphill battle regardless of how strong its content is.

4. Duplicate or Thin Content Across Pages

Multiple pages targeting near-identical keywords with barely differentiated content confuse Google about which page to rank — and often result in neither page ranking well. Consolidate overlapping pages into one strong, comprehensive resource instead.

5. Neglecting Page Speed

A slow site loses both rankings and visitors before they even see your content. See our companion article on website speed and SEO for the specific fixes that matter most.

6. No Consistent Review Strategy

Businesses that only ask for reviews sporadically miss out on both the trust signal and the ranking signal that a steady stream of recent reviews provides. Build review requests into your standard customer process, not an occasional afterthought.

The Real Cost of These Mistakes

None of these mistakes cause an overnight ranking collapse — they cause a slow, invisible ceiling on how high you can climb. Fixing even two or three of them is often enough to unlock meaningfully higher rankings within a few months.

7. Chasing Rankings Without Tracking Leads

Rankings are a means, not an end. Businesses that only track keyword position, without connecting it to actual enquiries and revenue, often can't tell whether their SEO investment is actually working — or where to focus next.
