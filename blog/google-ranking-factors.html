Google Ranking Factors Explained

Content, links, experience, technical health — Google uses hundreds of signals to rank pages. We break down the ones that actually matter and how to prioritize your SEO effort around them.

Google has confirmed it uses hundreds of ranking signals, and no one outside Google knows the exact weighting. But years of testing, documented Google guidance, and observed ranking patterns make it clear which categories matter most. Here's how to think about them.

Content Quality and Search Intent Match

The single biggest factor is whether your content genuinely answers what the searcher is looking for, at the depth and format they expect. A page stuffed with keywords but thin on real answers consistently underperforms a shorter page that directly and thoroughly satisfies intent.

E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust

Google's quality guidelines emphasize genuine experience and expertise, especially for topics that affect health, money, or safety. Author bylines, credentials, transparent business information, and real customer proof (reviews, case studies) all feed into how trustworthy Google — and your visitors — perceive your content to be.

Backlinks — Still a Core Signal

Links from relevant, authoritative sites remain one of the strongest ranking signals available. Quality dramatically outweighs quantity: one link from a respected, relevant local publication is worth more than dozens from low-quality directories.

Technical Health

Page speed, mobile usability, crawlability, and secure connections are baseline requirements. They rarely push a page to the very top on their own, but poor technical health can quietly cap how high great content is able to rank.

User Engagement Signals

How searchers interact with your result — whether they click, how long they stay, whether they return to search again — feeds back into Google's assessment of relevance and quality over time, rewarding pages that genuinely satisfy visitors.

How to Prioritize

Fix technical health first (it's a prerequisite, not a differentiator), then invest most heavily in content quality and genuine expertise, and build authoritative links as a longer-term compounding layer on top.

Local Ranking Factors Are Different

For local searches, proximity, Google Business Profile signals, and review quality carry additional weight beyond standard organic factors — see our dedicated guides on local SEO and Google Business Profile for the specifics.
