SEO Costs in the UK (2026 Pricing Guide)

Real, sourced pricing ranges by business size — what drives the difference, and how to compare proposals properly.

Quick Answer

Published 2026 UK industry pricing guides commonly report SEO monthly retainers ranging from roughly £750 to £3,500, with the overall market varying considerably by business size and competition level. Small businesses often spend £750–£2,000 per month; larger, more established businesses have been reported spending £5,000–£20,000 or more. Full-service retainers spanning multiple channels (SEO, PPC, social) run considerably higher, sometimes £3,500–£16,750 per month. These are indicative figures from published sources (cited below, accessed 20 July 2026), not a quote — see the breakdown below for what actually drives the difference.

Pricing by Business Size

  • Small businesses (roughly £100k–£500k revenue): commonly reported around £1,000–£2,500 per month.
  • Growing businesses (roughly £500k–£5M revenue): commonly reported around £2,500–£7,500 per month.
  • Established businesses (£5M+ revenue): commonly reported at £5,000–£20,000+ per month.

These bands reflect typical scope more than an arbitrary size-based markup — a larger business usually has more locations, more competitive keyword targets, and a larger site to maintain, all of which genuinely require more hours of work each month.

Common Pricing Models

  • Monthly retainer — the standard model for ongoing SEO, covering a defined scope of technical, content, and reporting work.
  • Project-based pricing — a fixed fee for a defined, time-limited piece of work, such as a technical audit or a migration.
  • Hourly consulting — common for freelance consultants or specific advisory engagements.
  • Performance-based pricing — fees tied to ranking or traffic milestones; treat with caution, as it can incentivise easily-gamed metrics over genuine outcomes.

Most established UK agencies default to a monthly retainer for the same reason it dominates elsewhere: SEO is an ongoing discipline, and rankings built up over months can erode if the work stops while competitors keep investing. Project-based engagements suit a narrowly defined need — a pre-launch technical audit, for instance — but rarely substitute for sustained work if durable visibility is the actual goal.

What a Professional Retainer Should Include

Regardless of pricing model, a professional UK SEO retainer should include: an initial technical and competitive audit, ongoing technical maintenance, content strategy tied to real UK search intent, digital PR and authority-building, local SEO where relevant, and genuine monthly reporting tied to business outcomes. A retainer missing several of these — particularly technical maintenance or authority-building — is a narrower service than the price might suggest, especially given how much UK rankings depend on genuine authority.

Authority-building deserves particular scrutiny in a UK retainer specifically, given how much weight it carries in this market. Ask exactly what form it takes — genuine digital PR and relationship-based outreach look very different, in both effort and effectiveness, from a line item that turns out to mean bulk directory submissions.

What Drives the Cost

  • Competition level — competitive national or London-based commercial terms cost considerably more to contest than regional or long-tail terms.
  • Site size and technical complexity — larger, more technically complex sites require more ongoing maintenance hours.
  • Authority-building intensity — genuine digital PR and link-earning work is resource-intensive and a significant driver of UK pricing specifically, given how competitive the market is.
  • Number of regions or locations — multi-region businesses need individually built local content and citations.
  • Reporting depth — genuine analysis and a real monthly conversation cost more than an automated export.

These factors compound rather than add independently. A national brand contesting competitive London-adjacent terms with a large, technically complex site and a genuine digital PR programme sits at the upper end of nearly every factor at once, which is consistent with the higher end of the published pricing ranges above.

The Risks of Cheap SEO

A price well below the published ranges above almost always means substantially less work — thinner content, minimal technical attention, little to no genuine authority-building. UK industry guides consistently flag budgets below roughly £1,000 a month as too thin for real strategic work. In the worst cases, unusually cheap SEO relies on tactics — spammy link building, thin content — that risk a Google penalty, and undoing that damage typically costs more than a properly scoped campaign would have from the start.

There's also a real opportunity cost specific to the UK's competitive intensity: months spent on an under-resourced campaign are months established competitors spend widening the authority gap further, which is exactly the dynamic this market punishes hardest.

How to Compare Proposals

Compare on scope, not just the headline monthly figure. Ask each agency to itemise what's included, how many hours the fee covers, and who specifically works on your account. Our guide to choosing an SEO company in the UK covers the fuller evaluation framework this pricing comparison should sit inside.

Watch for quotes that look competitive on price but bill separately for items a competing proposal already includes — content production or digital PR outreach, in particular. Ask directly whether the quoted figure is the full monthly cost before comparing numbers side by side.

How to Estimate Return on Investment

Estimate the value of a converted lead or sale for your business, estimate a realistic number of additional monthly leads a mature campaign could plausibly generate, and compare that recurring value against the retainer cost. This won't be precise in month one — UK SEO's return builds over months, especially given how competitive the market is — but it gives a working breakeven point to track the campaign against as it matures.

Questions to Ask Before Signing

  • What specifically is included in this monthly fee, itemised by activity?
  • How many hours does this retainer cover, and who's doing the work?
  • What deliverables should I expect within the first 90 days?
  • How does pricing change if scope increases?
  • Are there one-off setup fees on top of the monthly retainer?

FAQs

How much does SEO cost per month in the UK?

Industry pricing guides published in 2026 commonly report UK SEO retainers in the range of roughly £750 to £3,500 per month depending on business size and competition, with small businesses often spending £750-£2,000 and established businesses spending £5,000-£20,000 or more. These are indicative published ranges, not a quote.

Is £500 a month enough for SEO in the UK?

Most UK industry pricing guides flag budgets below roughly £1,000 a month as too thin for genuinely strategic work. Below that threshold, expect a narrow, largely maintenance-level service rather than a full campaign.

Why has UK SEO pricing increased recently?

Published industry sources report UK agency pricing up more than 30% compared to a few years prior, reflecting rising operating costs and increased competition for skilled SEO talent, among other factors.
